GAYBY BABY!
IN AUSTRALIA AND IN LONDON!

And speaking of Good Pitch² Australia... 2014 Alum Gayby Baby hit front page news last month when the Australian education minister banned the film from being screened in schools. Until that point, it was planned that the film would be screened as part of ‘Wear It Purple’, an event that supports the inclusion of LGBT pupils in schools. Well, if Success by Scandal really is a thing, then the cinema release of this film is going stratospheric!

The film is a beautiful portrait of four kids - Gus, Ebony, Matt and Graham - whose parents all happen to be gay. As they each wrestle with the challenges of oncoming adolescence, the outside world wrestles with the issue of marriage equality, and whether or not kids like them are at risk. We love this film, and are incredibly pleased that it's having its Australian release. If it's not coming to a cinema near you, then you can host a screening through Tugg!.

Meanwhile, over in the UK, Gayby Baby will get its European premiere at the BFI London Film Festival. Tickets are on sale, and may we recommend that you snag yourself a couple for one of their two screenings on Saturday October 10th and Monday October 12th!


GOOD PITCH² KENYA:
CALL FOR ENTRIES OPEN NOW!

In case you missed our July announcement, we're currently collaborating with DocuBox, the East African Documentary Film Fund, to produce Good Pitch² Kenya in 2016. Taking place in Nairobi in October 2016, Good Pitch² Kenya will present up to seven filmmaking teams to pitch their feature documentary and its associated audience engagement campaign in front of a live audience of funders and changemakers.

If you have connections to filmmakers or filmmaking networks in Africa, please do pass on details of this call for entries, which is open until November 2nd 2015. The DocuBox team are looking for independent film teams from across the African continent ready to join the 2015/16 programme:

- Films that tackle a significant national or global issue or have something important to show us about the world and ourselves
- Film teams who are looking for partnerships and funding to help their film create change around leading social and environmental issues
- Films that are currently in production and will be 60+ minutes in length when finished
- English-speaking filmmakers who are citizens of an African country

WHAT'S HAPPENING WITH OUR PEOPLE?

3.5 MINUTES, 10 BULLETS ON UK SCREENS

Bertha-BRITDOC grantee 3 1/2 Minutes, Ten Bullets tells the story of the shooting of black teenager Jordan Davis, and the subsequent trial of his murderer, Michael Dunn. Intercutting powerful footage from a riveting trial with intimate, observational scenes of Jordan's parents, Ron and Lucy, we see firsthand how difficult it is for them to grapple with unimaginable loss while fighting for justice for their son. The film integrates police interrogation footage, prison phone recordings and interviews with the others at the scene that night. The result is a powerful story about the devastating effects of racial bias, and the search for justice within the judicial system.

The film is coming to UK cinemas in October, but you can catch a preview of it at Picturehouse Central on Sept 30th. We recommend that you do.

THIS CHANGES EVERYTHING at TIFF

Another Good Pitch Chicago 2014 alum, This Changes Everything is directed by journalist and filmmaker Avi Lewis and produced in conjunction with Naomi Klein's bestselling book of the same name. The film is an urgent dispatch on climate change that contends that the greatest crisis we have ever faced also offers us the opportunity to address and correct the inhumane systems that have created it. It must be seen!

(T)ERROR: KICKSTARTER, LFF, US THEATRICAL!

After a barnstorming Kickstarter campaign that ensured the film would make it to our screens after all (the team have been hit with unprecedented legal bills that threatened the release), it's been announced that Good Pitch alum (T)ERROR will play the BFI London Film Festival (October 10th and October 12th), whilst at the same time it hits theaters across the US - what a month!

(T)ERROR is the first documentary to place filmmakers on the ground during a live FBI counterterrorism sting operation. With unprecedented access to an active informant, viewers get an unfettered glimpse of the government's counterterrorism tactics and the murky justifications behind them.

So far the film has been confirmed for NYC, Peoria, Houston, Dearborn, Pleasantville, Bellingham, Seattle and Los Angeles, with more to follow.

POITRAS AT NEW YORK FILM FESTIVAL!

New York Film Festival starts later this month and amongst the stellar film lineup, we have a couple of gems for you. First up, the world premiere of instalments of Laura Poitras' Bertha BRITDOC-supported Asylum, in which she shadows WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange as he publishes classified diplomatic cables and seeks asylum in London's Ecuadorian embassy. Find out more and get your ticket! Asylum is part of Field of Vision, the new documentary unit that was launched last week by dream team Poitras, AJ Schnack and Charlotte Cook - looking epic, above. Get the skinny on Field of Vision.

You can catch that on September 27th, when you can also see The Deeper They Bury Me, part of the Convergence program. An interactive encounter with one of America's most renowned political prisoners, the piece plunges users into the universe of Black Panther activist Herman Wallace, who was held in solitary for over 40 years at Louisiana's notorious Angola penitentiary. Written and directed by Angad Bhalla, who also directed the Good Pitch documentary Herman's House on the same subject, this new piece is a compelling portrait of defiance. Get your ticket!
